04
LLC Interest Sale vs. Traditional Deed Sale

At a sale price of $1,250,000, the structure of this transaction has meaningful consequences for both parties. A traditional deed sale triggers NJ Realty Transfer Tax, the Mansion Tax, full title insurance, and — most critically — the SR-1A filing that exposes the property to reassessment. An LLC interest transfer avoids most of these. The tables below show how the two structures compare, and what that means in dollars.

Benefits to the Seller — LLC Structure
Seller keeps $18,000–$22,000 at closing
The NJ Realty Transfer Tax (RTT) is a state tax paid by the seller every time real property transfers via deed. At $1,250,000 it runs $18k–$22k on a tiered scale — one of the highest transfer taxes in the country. On an LLC interest sale, no deed transfers, so RTT is never triggered. That money stays in the seller's pocket at closing.
Seller Saves
$18–22K
Buyer doesn't use Mansion Tax as a price reduction lever
On a deed sale over $1M, the buyer owes a 1% Mansion Tax — $12,500 at this price. In almost every transaction, the buyer factors this into their net cost and uses it to justify a lower offer or a closing credit. An LLC sale eliminates the Mansion Tax entirely, removing that negotiating pressure on the seller's price.
Protects
$12,500
Faster, simpler close
No title search on the real property, no lender appraisal fight on a mixed-use building, no deed recording delays. An LLC interest transfer is a private transaction — cleaner paperwork, fewer third parties, and typically a faster path from executed PSA to closing.
Benefit
Speed
Potential capital gains deferral flexibility
Depending on how the LLC is structured, an interest sale may allow the seller to negotiate installment sale treatment or other tax structuring that a deed sale does not permit as cleanly. This is seller-specific and requires CPA guidance, but the optionality exists under an LLC structure and not under a standard deed transfer.
Benefit
Tax Planning

An LLC interest sale requires thorough buyer due diligence on the entity — tax returns, liabilities, liens, open permits, violations, and operating agreements. Green Ventures is prepared to conduct this review. Both parties should engage a NJ-licensed real estate attorney and CPA before structuring the transaction.
